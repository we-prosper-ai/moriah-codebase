## What You're Getting

Finance Friend is a tool built specifically for people like you—people who earn real money, pay real taxes, and want to actually understand what's happening with your finances without judgment.

**What it does:**
- Takes your bank statements and shows you what's really happening with your money
- Answers your questions about your finances in plain English
- Helps you understand your spending patterns
- Prepares you for tax season
- Gives you judgment-free guidance

**What it doesn't do:**
- Connect to your bank (you upload your statement, we never see your login)
- Replace your accountant or financial advisor (we complement them)
- Give you investment advice (not our lane)
- Charge you anything during beta (free access through March 31)

## How to Get Started (5 Minutes)

### Step 1: Create Your Account
1. Go to [beta.financefriend.co](https://beta.financefriend.co)
2. Click "Sign Up"
3. Enter your email and create a password
4. Check your email and confirm (if required)

### Step 2: Gather Your Bank Statement
You'll need one of these:
- PDF from your bank (usually from your online account)
- CSV export from your bank
- Or a spreadsheet with columns: Date, Description, Amount

**Don't have it?** Most banks let you export statements:
- **Chase**: Statements → Download as CSV
- **Bank of America**: Account → Export transactions
- **Wells Fargo**: Accounts → Download statements
- **Independent bank**: Look for "Export" or "Download"

### Step 3: Upload Your Statement
1. Click "Upload Statement" on the dashboard
2. Choose your file (PDF or CSV)
3. Wait 10-30 seconds while we process it
4. Done! Your transactions are now in Finance Friend

### Step 4: Explore Your Data
You'll see:
- **All your transactions** in a searchable table
- **Spending summary** by category
- **Monthly trends** (vs. last month)
- **Insights** about your spending patterns

## What to Do Next

### Spend 5 Minutes Exploring

- **Change a category:** Click on a transaction's category, select a new one. Finance Friend learns your patterns.
- **Search your spending:** Use the search box to find "coffee" or "amazon" or any merchant.
- **Filter by date:** Select a date range to focus on a specific week or month.

### Spend 5 Minutes Talking to Finance Friend

1. Click the **Chat** button (bottom right)
2. Ask something real:
   - "How much did I spend on groceries?"
   - "What's my biggest expense category?"
   - "Did my spending change from last month?"
   - "Am I saving enough?"
3. Read the response and the data it's citing

### Spend 10 Minutes Giving Feedback

When you're done exploring:
1. Look for the feedback button (usually a smiley face icon)
2. Tell us what you think:
   - What worked well?
   - What's confusing?
   - What's missing?
   - Would you actually use this?

**This feedback is gold.** We use it to decide what to build next.

## Common Questions During Beta

## Feature Walkthrough

### Dashboard

**What you see:**
- Total income (how much came in)
- Total expenses (how much went out)
- Net (the difference)
- Breakdown by category (pie chart or list)

**What you can do:**
- Change the date range (this month, last quarter, all time)
- Click a category to see transactions in that category
- See trends (↑ up, ↓ down, → flat) vs previous period

**Why it matters:**
You get a single view of your actual money situation instead of scattered bank accounts and vague feelings.

### Transactions

**What you see:**
- Every transaction with date, description, amount, category
- Sortable by any column
- Searchable
- Filterable by category or date

**What you can do:**
- Click a transaction to edit its category
- Change the category if Finance Friend got it wrong
- Add notes ("This was a business trip")
- Delete a transaction (if you uploaded it twice)

**Why it matters:**
Wrong categories = wrong insights. Fix a few and Finance Friend learns your patterns. Next month it's smarter.

### Chat

**What you see:**
- A conversation box at the bottom right
- Your questions on the right
- Finance Friend's answers on the left

**What you can do:**
- Ask anything about your finances
- Get plain-English answers backed by your actual data
- Continue a conversation thread
- Start a new conversation

**Try asking:**
- "How much did I spend on [category]?"
- "What's my biggest spending category?"
- "How does this month compare to last month?"
- "What's my spending trend?"
- "Should I be concerned about X?"

**Why it matters:**
You get answers without having to dig through spreadsheets or do mental math. And the tone is warm, non-judgmental, and actually helpful.
