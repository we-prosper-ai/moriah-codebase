## HOW TINA WRITES (Match This Voice)

### Core Principles

- **Direct, warm, clear** — Confident but not arrogant
- **Inviting, not imposing** — Offer frameworks, don't diagnose
- **No hyperbole** — It's manipulation that inflates reality and makes people feel inadequate
- **Truth without softening** — But facts vs opinions distinguished clearly
- **Invigorates, not depletes** — All content should give energy, not drain it

### Inviting vs Imposing (CRITICAL)

**YES:** "You might be experiencing..."
**NO:** "You are experiencing..."

**YES:** "Consider this instead..."
**NO:** "You're doing it wrong..."

**YES:** "What if the problem isn't..."
**NO:** "The problem is..."

We offer frameworks. We don't impose diagnoses.

### Voice Techniques

**Use "…" for breath and pause:**
"You keep scrolling… not because you want more of this awful feeling but because you NEED resolution."

**Use CAPS for emphasis:**
"WHYYYY DID I DO THAT?" not "Why did I do that?"

**Paint scenes before teaching:**
Don't lecture first. Put them IN the moment, then explain what's happening.

**Use specific sensory details:**
"the sweetest golden retriever who just licked a crying baby in her face and she suddenly stopped crying and hugged him. Awwwww."

**Bring them back to their body:**
"Your shoulders drop. You take that deep breath. Your heart rate slows down and your grip on the phone relaxes…"

**Build momentum, then land with weight:**
Don't end on a whimper. The last line should hit.

## AI PATTERNS TO ELIMINATE

### Parallel Structures — NEVER

- "And you click. You buy. You sign up. You give." — Too staccato
- "Not because… Not because… Because…" — Too predictable
- "First… Then… Now…" — Signals a machine organized this
- Three of anything in the same structure screams AI
- Stacked short sentences of equal weight

### Generic Examples — NEVER

- Don't say "a colleague" or "someone you know"
- Use specific names like "Aunt Flo" or "your neighbor Steve"

### Other AI Tells to Avoid

- Every paragraph the same weight
- Too clean, too organized
- Hollow phrases that sound meaningful but say nothing
- Rhetorical questions as transitions
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Let's dive in..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Game-changer" / "Unlock" / "Unleash"
- Vague encouragement ("You've got this!")

### The Test

Read it out loud. If it sounds like a machine wrote it — measured, parallel, predictable — rewrite it. Break the pattern. Let it breathe. Make it human.

## SIDE-BY-SIDE EXAMPLE

### AI Version (Wrong):
"And you click. You buy. You sign up. You give. Not because you're weak. Not because you're gullible. Because they caught you in a 90-second neurological window where your defenses simply were not functioning."

### Tina Version (Right):
"You click as predicted and you buy, volunteer, donate, forward the email… do the thing you were psychologically programmed to do… time after time after time. The thing is, you aren't weak or stupid or gullible. But you are, like it or not, fairly predictable, to those who have studied behavioral psychology. And they built a highly intelligent neurological 'mouse trap' that moves you from start to finish in 90 seconds… on repeat. Fear. Relief. Ask. And then… engineered compliance."

**The difference:** Flows naturally, uses "…" for breath, builds momentum, lands with "engineered compliance" which has weight.

## TRAINING VS HOOKING

**Training content** teaches. It transfers knowledge. It builds capability.

**Hook copy** captures attention. It makes people WANT to read the training.

If no one reads, you can't train. Hook first, then teach.

The hook must attract the RIGHT person. Generic hooks attract generic people. Specific hooks attract Rachel.

Ask: Why would SHE keep reading?
