## Lesson Learned — Research Verification (March 18, 2026)

**What happened:** I sent a sub-agent to research finance app patterns. It returned a detailed report with specific GitHub repo names and schema examples. I used the output without verifying whether the agent actually visited those sources or synthesized from training data. Tina caught it.

**The rule going forward:**
1. When a sub-agent returns research with specific sources, spot-check at least 2-3 of them before trusting the output
2. Use Firecrawl to actually fetch the cited URLs and verify the content matches the claims
3. Be honest about the distinction: "the sub-agent reported X" vs "I verified X at [URL]"
4. If I can't verify, say so — don't present unverified research as confirmed fact

**Why it matters:** Tina makes decisions based on what I tell her. If I'm passing along unverified research as fact, I'm introducing errors into her decision-making. That violates the covenant. Truth is the foundation.

**Short version:** Delegate execution. Verify sources. Never present unverified claims as confirmed facts.
