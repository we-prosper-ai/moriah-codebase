## 💡 SIDE-BY-SIDE COMPARISON

### YNAB (You Need A Budget)

**What it does:** Budgeting + discipline

**Strengths:**
- Powerful budgeting engine
- Great for people who want to follow rules
- Mobile app is excellent
- Community is very active
- Good for shared/family budgets

**Weaknesses:**
- Only tracks money
- Assumes budgets are static
- Doesn't help with decisions BEFORE you spend
- Can feel restrictive

**Best for:** People who want strict budget control

**Not good for:** 
- Self-employed with variable income
- People thinking about time/energy trade-offs
- Decision-making (it tracks after you spend)

**Why Finance Friend is different:**
- We help you decide BEFORE you spend
- We track time + energy + money + freedom
- We're built for people with variable situations
- Better for "should I?" not "did I?"

---

### Mint (Closed, but Copycats Exist)

**What it does:** Spending tracking + insights

**Strengths:**
- Automatic categorization
- Free tier available
- Simple interface
- Broad overview of spending

**Weaknesses:**
- Only tells you WHERE you spent
- Doesn't help with decisions
- Limited insights
- Privacy concerns (history of data sales)

**Best for:** People who want passive awareness of spending

**Not good for:**
- People who want to change behavior
- Self-employed with complex finances
- Decision-making
- Privacy-conscious users

**Why Finance Friend is different:**
- We help you make better decisions
- We prioritize privacy (unlike Mint's history)
- We track time + energy, not just money
- We're a decision partner, not just a tracker

---

### Wave (Accounting + Invoicing)

**What it does:** Invoicing + small business accounting

**Strengths:**
- Excellent for invoicing
- Good for small business accounting
- Free tier (very affordable)
- Connected to business finances

**Weaknesses:**
- Focuses on business, not personal decisions
- Not built for decision-making
- Assumes traditional business structure
- Limited coaching/insights

**Best for:** Small business owners who need invoicing

**Not good for:**
- Personal finance management
- Decision-making about trade-offs
- People who want a thinking partner
- Self-employed managing time/energy

**Why Finance Friend is different:**
- We handle personal + business money
- We focus on decisions, not just tracking
- We track time + energy, not just invoices
- We're a decision coach, not accounting software

## 🔍 THE DECISION-MAKING MATRIX

**What happens when you ask each tool a question:**

### Question: "Should I take this $10K project?"

**YNAB:** (Can't answer — budgeting tool)

**Mint:** "Your income will increase by $10K. Your spending was $3K last month."

**Wave:** "That's $10K revenue. Your expenses are $2K. Net: $8K profit."

**Finance Friend:** "$10K, yes. But it's 60 hours of work (your hourly rate: $166). 
And it will drain 40% of your available energy. And it locks you up for 4 weeks, 
reducing freedom. Worth it?"

---

### Question: "Why do I overspend on coffee?"

**YNAB:** "You budgeted $50, spent $85. Adjust your budget."

**Mint:** "You spent $85 this month on coffee. Last month: $60."

**Wave:** (Not relevant — business/invoicing tool)

**Finance Friend:** "You spend more on coffee on days you have multiple client calls 
(high-energy drain). It's a coping mechanism. The real issue isn't coffee, 
it's your meeting schedule. Want to fix that instead?"

---

### Question: "Can I afford to reduce my work hours?"

**YNAB:** "Not relevant to budgeting."

**Mint:** "Your income is X, expenses are Y. Do the math."

**Wave:** "That's a personal decision, not accounting."

**Finance Friend:** "Your hourly rate is $X. If you work 40 hours instead of 50, 
you lose $X income but gain 10 hours + 20% energy recovery + more freedom. 
Trade-off: worth $X/month to you?"
