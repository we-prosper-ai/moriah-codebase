# Script: Your Currency Baseline

## Opening Hook (1:40)

I want to tell you about something that changed how I think about money.

Six months ago, I met with a coaching client—let's call her Sarah. Sarah's been running her business for four years. She's a photographer. And when we looked at her finances, I was stunned. She's making $180,000 a year.

Now, that's a good income. Most people would be thrilled.

But here's what else I learned: Sarah is working 65 hours a week.

Sixty. Five. Hours.

She's not taking a real day off. She's checking email at 11 PM. She's missing her kids' soccer games. She's exhausted. And she told me something I'll never forget: "Tina, I'm making more money than I ever have, but I feel more trapped than ever. How is this possible?"

And it hit me that we've been measuring wealth all wrong.

We think wealth is a single number—your bank account. But for Sarah, that number was lying. Because she bought that money with something much more valuable: her time. Her energy. Her freedom.

That conversation changed everything for me, because I realized: **wealth isn't about how much money you have. It's about the ratio of your four currencies matching what you actually value.**

And that's what we're diving into today.

---

## Teaching Section (4:20)

### The Four Currencies Framework

Let me introduce you to the Four Currencies. These are the real measures of wealth that matter.

The first is **Money**—the obvious one. But money alone is not wealth. Money buys things. Sometimes it buys freedom. Sometimes it buys a prison.

The second is **Time**. Time is the one currency you can't make more of. You have 168 hours this week. That's it. No matter how much money you make, you can't buy more time. You can only trade it. And if you're trading hours for dollars at $12 an hour (like Sarah was doing), then no amount of income saves you.

The third is **Energy**. This is the one nobody talks about, and it's why so many people who have money are absolutely miserable. Energy is what lets you use your time and money to build things. When you're depleted—mentally, emotionally, physically—no amount of time or money fixes it. You can have two weeks off and feel zero better because your energy is gone. I know this because I've lived it.

And the fourth is **Freedom**—the result when the other three are aligned.

Freedom is what Sarah actually wanted. Not more money. Freedom.

### Why This Matters to You

If you're watching this, you probably have an entrepreneurial spirit. Maybe you're building a business. Maybe you're coaching. Maybe you're creating. And you probably felt, at some point, what Sarah felt: confusion. You're making money, but it doesn't feel like wealth.

That's because you're living with a mismatch in your four currencies.

And here's the thing: **you can't fix a mismatch you can't see.**

So today, we're going to make it visible.

### The Four Currencies in Action

Let me give you some real examples.

Sarah's problem was clear once we looked at it: She had money and time, but they weren't in the right ratio. She was trading 65 hours of her week for $180,000. That's about $54 an hour after accounting for taxes and expenses. And more importantly, she had zero energy left. She was running on fumes.

Now compare that to another client, Marcus. Marcus is a consultant. He charges $250 an hour. But here's what's interesting: Marcus works 20 hours a week. He has money, yes. But he also has time. He also still has energy because he's not exhausted. He's built his business so that the four currencies are somewhat aligned. Not perfectly—he'd like more freedom, less structure—but much better than Sarah.

And then there's Jordan, who I coached last year. Jordan's in the opposite place. Jordan has energy, has time—works on his own schedule, does what he loves. But he has almost no money. And that creates a different kind of problem. Because without money, his freedom is limited. He can't buy back time. He can't invest. He's stuck.

So wealth—true wealth—is when you have enough money AND enough time AND enough energy AND the freedom to make choices.

**The question becomes: what's YOUR ratio?**

### Your Baseline Matters

Why do I care about your baseline? Because you can't move toward something if you don't know where you're starting.

In the next part of this lesson, we're going to do an exercise. You're going to rate each of your four currencies right now, today, in your life. Not where you want to be. Where you ARE.

And I know that might feel uncomfortable. But here's what happens when you measure:

You see the mismatch. Once you see it, you can't unsee it. And once you can't unsee it, you can fix it.

This is how I went from being exhausted and overwhelmed to building a life where I work 6 hours a week and make more money than I ever have.

Not because I found some magic hack. But because I saw the mismatch, and I fixed it systematically.

So let's start with your baseline.

---

## Application Section (1:20)

### Your Currency Assessment Exercise

You're going to see a workbook exercise. This is yours to do in your own time—and I encourage you to actually do it, not just watch. The value is in the reflection.

Here's what you'll do:

**First:** Rate yourself on each currency, 1-10.

- Money: How financially stable do you feel? (1 = financially stressed, 10 = completely secure)
- Time: How much discretionary time do you have? (1 = no free time, 10 = abundant free time)
- Energy: How energized are you most days? (1 = depleted, 10 = thriving)
- Freedom: How much control do you have over your choices? (1 = none, 10 = complete autonomy)

**Second:** Look at those numbers. Most people will see one or two that are way lower than the others. That's your mismatch.

**Third:** Ask yourself: "Why? Why is Time low but Money high? Why is Energy low? What's creating that pattern?"

You don't need to answer me. You need to answer yourself. But notice it.

That mismatch is the thing we're going to untangle over the next few weeks.

---

## Closing Section (0:40)

What we just did here is the foundation. You now know the framework. You've measured yourself against it.

But measuring is just the beginning.

Over the next two weeks, you're going to dive deep into each currency. You'll learn exactly what's creating your mismatch and what to do about it.

Here's what's possible: You can build a business, build a life, where you have money AND time AND energy AND freedom. Not someday. Not eventually. But actually.

Sarah did it—she restructured her pricing, she hired her first contractor, and within six months, she went from 65 hours to 25 hours, same income, way more energy. That's what you're capable of.

Marcus is showing us you can have all four currencies in balance.

And Jordan's learning what it looks like to layer in money without losing the freedom and energy he already has.

You're in good company. And you're in the right place.

So I'll see you in the next lesson. We're going deep into Time—the most honest currency.

## Visual Direction

### Opening Hook (0:00-1:40)

**Visuals:**
- Warm, intimate setting (your office or coaching space)
- You sitting naturally (not behind a desk—on a stool or chair)
- Camera close (personal, conversational distance)
- Warm lighting (overhead softbox + side key)

**Text Overlays:**
- No text in opening (immerse in story)
- At story beat "Sarah": Name appears briefly
- At framework intro: Nothing yet (let story land)

**Tone:**
- You're telling a friend about something important
- Conversational, not rehearsed
- Pause after "that conversation changed everything"

### Teaching Section (1:40-6:00)

**Visuals (per subsection):**

#### Four Currencies Framework (1:40-3:30)
- B-roll of four things: Clock (time), Money (bills), Person resting (energy), Person free/hiking (freedom)
- As you introduce each, show visual for 10 seconds
- Text overlay: "Money", "Time", "Energy", "Freedom" (clean typography)
- Color-coded: Each currency gets a color from palette
- Return to you for explanation

#### Why This Matters (3:30-4:30)
- More personal footage (you working, you with clients)
- Quick clips of different scenarios
- Show the confusion/problem clearly
- Warm, empathetic tone in visuals (not clinical)

#### Four Currencies in Action (4:30-6:00)
- Three small case study sections:
  - Sarah: High money + time, low energy (show exhaustion visually)
  - Marcus: Balanced (show peace)
  - Jordan: Energy + time, low money (show struggle)
- Use graphics, not actors (abstract + authentic)
- Show the ratio concept with simple visual metaphor (scales, bars, something intuitive)

### Application Section (6:00-7:20)

**Visuals:**
- Split screen: You on left, workbook exercise on right
- Show the assessment form clearly (readable)
- Walk through with pointing/gestures
- Slow pacing (give them time to think)
- Warm, encouraging tone

**Text Overlays:**
- "Rate yourself 1-10"
- "Money: Financial stability"
- "Time: Discretionary time"
- "Energy: How energized?"
- "Freedom: Control over choices"

### Closing Section (7:20-8:00)

**Visuals:**
- Back to intimate setting (you, camera close)
- You looking slightly into distance (reflective, hopeful)
- Maybe your hand on heart (connection)
- Final shot: You smiling (warmth, invitation to next lesson)

**Text Overlays:**
- At "what's possible": Bold text
- "Next lesson: The Time Currency"
- Course progress bar (1 of 12)

## Voice Specifications

### Opening Hook
- **Emotion:** Warm, intimate, slightly vulnerable
- **Pace:** Natural, conversational (not rushed)
- **Energy:** Medium intensity—you're drawing them in
- **Tone Shifts:**
  - Story opening: Curious, warm
  - "She's making $180K": Slight tone shift to admiration
  - "65 hours a week": Slower, more deliberate
  - Story climax: Empathetic, understanding
  - Framework intro: Thoughtful, authoritative

### Teaching Section
- **Emotion:** Authoritative but humble ("Here's what I've learned")
- **Pace:** Clear and deliberate (they're taking mental notes)
- **Energy:** Higher than opening—this is the value delivery
- **Tone Shifts:**
  - Framework intro: Educational, clear
  - Each currency: Slightly different tone
    - Money: Matter-of-fact
    - Time: Serious (this is important)
    - Energy: Warm (relatable struggle)
    - Freedom: Hopeful (this is why it matters)
  - Case studies: Empathetic, specific

### Application Section
- **Emotion:** Encouraging, practical
- **Pace:** Slower here—let concepts land
- **Energy:** Settling (less intense than teaching)
- **Pauses:** Pause after each question for them to reflect

### Closing Section
- **Emotion:** Hopeful, inviting
- **Pace:** Natural, warm
- **Energy:** Lower—reflective
- **Tone:** Personal, forward-looking
