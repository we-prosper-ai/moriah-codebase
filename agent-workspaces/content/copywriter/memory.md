## Style Guide

### Core Principles
1. **Lead with transformation** — Not features, but what customers become
2. **Use power words** — Specificity beats generality ("3x faster" beats "faster")
3. **Test variations** — Create A/B alternatives, let data decide
4. **Value delivery focus** — Every word should serve the customer, not the product

### Copy Framework

#### Sales Page Structure
```
HEADLINE
- Lead with biggest transformation
- Specific benefit, not clever wordplay
- Make reader curious

SUBHEADING
- Clarify who this is for
- Address primary objection
- Build on headline

PROBLEM
- Show you understand the pain
- Use customer's language
- Make them feel seen

SOLUTION
- Introduce your approach
- Show it's different from others
- Build credibility

PROOF
- Testimonials with specific results
- Case studies with numbers
- Social proof (X customers using it)

DETAILS
- What exactly do they get?
- What does transformation look like?
- Timeline to results

OBJECTIONS & FAQ
- Address hesitations directly
- "But will this work for me?"
- "What if I don't have X background?"

CTA
- Clear next step
- Remove friction
- Create urgency if appropriate

GUARANTEE
- Risk reversal
- Show confidence
- Make saying "yes" easy
```

#### Email Sequence Formula (5-email launch sequence)
1. **Email 1 (Day 1)** — Story + Problem identification
2. **Email 2 (Day 2)** — Solution intro + Why it matters
3. **Email 3 (Day 3)** — Proof + Testimonials
4. **Email 4 (Day 4)** — Objection handling
5. **Email 5 (Day 5)** — Final CTA + Urgency

## Conversion Psychology Principles

### Headlines That Work
- Lead with transformation, not feature
- Use numbers where possible ("3x faster" > "much faster")
- Address specific customer ("For busy entrepreneurs" > "For everyone")
- Show what success looks like

### Email Open Rates (Subject Line Tactics)
- Curiosity gap: "You won't believe what [X] costs now"
- Specificity: "[3 reasons] your AI agent isn't [problem]"
- Personalization: "[First name], here's your [customization]"
- Urgency: "Only 48 hours left: [limited offer]"

### Conversion Killers to Avoid
- Vague benefits ("Improve your business")
- Corporate speak ("Leverage synergies")
- Empty superlatives ("Best-in-class", "World-class")
- Asking for too much commitment upfront
- Unclear CTAs

### Language Patterns That Convert
- "Imagine if you could..." (paint picture)
- "Here's what [successful customers] did..." (social proof)
- "In the next X minutes..." (time boundary)
- "Here's the truth about..." (transparency)
- "You're probably wondering..." (acknowledge objection)
