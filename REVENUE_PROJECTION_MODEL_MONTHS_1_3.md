## What Drives Better Outcomes (Levers You Control)

### Lever 1: Email Launch Quality
- **Conservative:** 500 emails, 40% open, 10% click, 30% signup = 15 conversions
- **If you improve:** 700 emails, 50% open, 15% click, 40% signup = 42 conversions
- **Impact:** +27 premium conversions = +$270/month

### Lever 2: Paid Acquisition
- Small ad spend ($200-500/month) can 2-3x signups in month 2+
- At $5-10 CAC (cost to acquire), break-even in 3-5 months of premium subscription
- But only works if your product is actually good (retention matters)

### Lever 3: Community / Word-of-Mouth
- Every 10 premium users who love it = ~2-3 new signups (referral rate)
- At 20 premium users month 1 = potential 4-6 referral signups month 2
- Compounds if you keep retention high

### Lever 4: v3 Launch Timing
- v3 is higher-priced ($19.99 vs. $9.99)
- Can attract premium segment of v2 audience
- v2 users upgrading = extra revenue
- v3 launch in month 2-3 could add $500-1,000/month

### Lever 5: Product-Market Fit Validation
- The #1 determinant of success is: Is this solving a real problem?
- Month 1 feedback will tell you
- If conversion rate is <5%, product may not be resonating
- If conversion rate is >15%, you might be underselling

## The Numbers That Matter Most

**Watch these 5 metrics obsessively:**

1. **Conversion Rate:** % of signups → premium
   - Target: 10-15%
   - Red flag: <5% (product not selling)
   - Green light: >20% (goldmine)

2. **Churn Rate:** % of premium users who cancel
   - Target: <5% monthly (expected in beta)
   - Red flag: >10% (people don't value it)
   - Green light: <2% (people love it)

3. **CAC (Cost to Acquire):** How much you spend per customer
   - If organic: $0 (free)
   - If paid ads: $5-15 (aim for <$5)
   - Payback: Recoup CAC in 3-5 months (3 × $9.99 = $30)

4. **LTV (Lifetime Value):** How much profit per customer over lifetime
   - Conservative: 12 months × $9.99 = $120 per customer
   - With repeat upgrades: $150+
   - Rule: LTV should be >3× CAC

5. **Growth Rate:** Month-over-month growth
   - Month 1→2: Target 50-100% growth
   - Month 2→3: Target 25-50% growth
   - If declining: Problem (check retention)
